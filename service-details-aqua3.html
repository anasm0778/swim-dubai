AQUA3 – Skill Development & Stroke Introduction

Stroke Basics, Breath Control & Endurance

This level is for swimmers who are confident in the water and are ready to develop basic swimming strokes, improve breath control, and increase distance and endurance with more independence.

Key Learning Milestones

  • Breath Control & Submersion

    Blow bubbles for 5 seconds while swimming, exhale underwater while moving, and pick up object from chest-deep water.

  • Floating & Body Position

    Front and back glides for 3–5 seconds, and transition between floats independently with control and awareness.

  • Travel & Kicking

    Swim 5m on front and back using arms and legs, and kick 5–10m on front and back with rhythm and control.

  • Stroke Introduction

    Learn front crawl and backstroke arm movements, and begin breaststroke leg action with float or wall support.

Why Choose AQUA3?

AQUA3 builds upon foundational water confidence to introduce stroke techniques and breath control. Children learn to coordinate movement, manage breathing underwater, and swim with independence and control.

With expert coaching and structured progress, swimmers gain endurance and technique that set the stage for advanced swimming success.
